Title: Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Know Your Customer (KYC) Policy
Category: legal
Doc-ID: legal-005

Nexus Bank is committed to preventing money laundering, terrorist financing, and financial crime. This summary explains our AML/KYC obligations and what they mean for you as a customer.

What is KYC?
Know Your Customer (KYC) is the process of verifying the identity of our customers. We are required by law to collect and verify:
- Your full legal name, date of birth, and address.
- A government-issued photo ID (passport, national ID, or driver's license).
- For business accounts: Ownership structure, ultimate beneficial owners, and business registration documents.

Why we ask for documents:
We are legally required under the Bank Secrecy Act (BSA), USA PATRIOT Act, and other regulations to verify identities and monitor transactions. Providing accurate information is your legal obligation as a customer.

Ongoing monitoring:
We continuously monitor account activity for suspicious patterns, including:
- Unusually large or frequent cash transactions.
- Rapid movement of funds through accounts.
- Transactions inconsistent with your stated purpose or income.
- Transactions involving high-risk countries or sanctioned entities.

What happens if suspicious activity is detected:
- We may request additional documentation or explanation.
- In serious cases, we are legally required to file a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) with FinCEN (Financial Crimes Enforcement Network). We cannot disclose to you if a SAR has been filed.
- We may restrict or close the account pending investigation.

Your responsibilities:
- Provide accurate and up-to-date information.
- Notify us promptly of any changes to your identity, address, or business structure.
- Do not conduct or facilitate any transaction that constitutes money laundering or fraud.

For questions about our AML/KYC requirements, contact compliance@nexusbank.com.
