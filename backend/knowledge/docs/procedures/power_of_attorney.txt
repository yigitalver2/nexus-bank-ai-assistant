Title: Power of Attorney — Banking Authority
Category: procedures
Doc-ID: proc-008

A Power of Attorney (POA) allows a designated person (the "attorney" or agent) to manage your Nexus Bank accounts on your behalf.

When is a POA needed?
- If you are traveling abroad for an extended period.
- If you are ill or incapacitated.
- If you want a trusted person (e.g., spouse, parent) to manage your finances.

Types of POA accepted:
- General POA: Grants broad banking authority (transfers, payments, account management).
- Limited POA: Grants authority for specific transactions or a defined period.
- Lasting/Durable POA: Remains valid even if the account holder loses mental capacity (must be registered with the relevant authority).

How to register a POA:

1. Obtain a legally executed POA document (signed by you and notarized or witnessed as required by your jurisdiction).
2. Both you (the donor) and the attorney must visit a Nexus Bank branch together (or the attorney alone if you are unable, with the original POA and their own photo ID).
3. Complete the "Third Party Authority" form at the branch.
4. The branch will verify the document and set up access.

Processing time: 3-5 business days for the authority to be fully active.

Important notes:
- A POA does not remove your own access to the account.
- You can revoke a POA at any time in writing or in person at a branch.
- Nexus Bank is not responsible for decisions made by the attorney acting within the scope of the granted authority.
