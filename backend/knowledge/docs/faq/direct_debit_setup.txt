Title: Setting Up Direct Debit and Standing Orders
Category: faq
Doc-ID: faq-008

Direct debits allow third parties (e.g., utility companies, subscriptions) to automatically collect payments from your account on agreed dates. Standing orders are fixed recurring payments you set up yourself.

Setting up a Direct Debit:
- The company collecting payment will send you a Direct Debit Mandate form.
- Sign the mandate and return it to the company — they notify the bank directly.
- You will see the direct debit listed under My Accounts > Direct Debits within 3 business days.

Setting up a Standing Order:
1. Log in to online banking and go to Payments > Standing Orders.
2. Click "New Standing Order."
3. Enter the recipient IBAN, amount, start date, frequency (weekly, monthly, etc.), and optional end date.
4. Confirm with your transaction password or 2FA code.

Cancelling a Direct Debit or Standing Order:
- Online: Payments > Direct Debits / Standing Orders > Cancel.
- Note: Cancelling a direct debit at the bank does not cancel your contract with the company. Contact the company separately.

If a direct debit causes your balance to go below zero and you do not have an overdraft facility, the payment will be rejected and you may incur a failed payment fee of $5.
