Title: Freezing and Unfreezing Your Account
Category: faq
Doc-ID: faq-018

Account freezing is different from card blocking. When an account is frozen, no transactions (incoming or outgoing) can be processed.

Why an account might be frozen:
- You requested a temporary freeze (e.g., suspected unauthorized access).
- The bank detected suspicious activity and froze the account for your protection.
- Legal or regulatory hold (e.g., court order).

How to freeze your account (self-initiated):
1. Log in to online banking and go to My Accounts > Account Settings.
2. Select "Freeze Account."
3. Confirm with your 2FA code.
The freeze takes effect immediately.

How to unfreeze your account (self-initiated):
1. Log in and go to My Accounts > Account Settings.
2. Select "Unfreeze Account" and confirm.
If you cannot log in, call 1-800-NEXUS-00 — we will verify your identity before unfreezing.

Bank-initiated freeze:
- You will be notified by SMS and email if the bank freezes your account.
- Call 1-800-NEXUS-00 to understand the reason and resolve it.
- Common reasons: suspected fraud, unresolved compliance document requests, or court order.

During a freeze:
- Pending direct debits will be declined.
- Loan repayments due will not be processed (late payment fees may still apply — contact us to arrange payment via another channel).
