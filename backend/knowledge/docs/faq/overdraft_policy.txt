Title: Overdraft Policy and Facility
Category: faq
Doc-ID: faq-017

An overdraft allows your account to go below zero up to a pre-approved limit, so that payments are not rejected when your balance is insufficient.

Types of overdraft:
- Arranged overdraft: Pre-approved limit set on your account. Interest applies only on the amount used.
- Unarranged overdraft: Going below zero without a pre-approved limit. Higher fees apply and may result in payment rejection.

Applying for an arranged overdraft:
- Log in to online banking and go to My Accounts > Overdraft.
- Select the account and apply for a limit (up to $5,000 for standard accounts, $15,000 for business).
- Approval is subject to a credit check and may take 1-2 business days.

Interest rates:
- Arranged overdraft: 15.9% APR.
- Unarranged overdraft: 39.9% APR + $5 daily fee (max $15/month).

Paying back an overdraft:
- Deposit funds into the account. The overdraft is reduced automatically.
- You are not required to make a fixed monthly repayment, but interest accrues daily.

Cancelling an overdraft facility:
- Go to My Accounts > Overdraft > Cancel Facility.
- The overdraft must be fully repaid before cancellation is complete.

Customers with an existing overdraft who miss the arranged limit will have payments declined to protect them from excessive debt.
