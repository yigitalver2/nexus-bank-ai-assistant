Title: Phishing, Smishing, and Vishing — How to Spot and Report Scams
Category: security
Doc-ID: sec-003

Fraudsters use phishing (email), smishing (SMS), and vishing (phone calls) to steal banking credentials. Here is how to recognize and avoid these attacks.

Common phishing tactics:

Email phishing:
- Fake emails that look like they are from Nexus Bank, asking you to "verify your account" or "confirm a suspicious transaction."
- These emails often contain urgent language ("Your account will be suspended in 24 hours") and a link to a fake website.
- The fake website looks identical to nexusbank.com but has a slightly different URL (e.g., nexus-bank-secure.com, nexus.bank-login.com).

Smishing (SMS phishing):
- A text message claiming to be from Nexus Bank with a link to confirm a transaction or unlock your account.
- Real Nexus Bank SMS messages never include clickable links to log in.

Vishing (phone call fraud):
- A caller pretending to be a Nexus Bank representative asking for your PIN, OTP, or online banking password.
- Sometimes they claim your account is under attack and urgently need your details to "protect" it.

How to protect yourself:
- Never click links in unsolicited emails or SMS messages. Go directly to nexusbank.com by typing it in your browser.
- Never share your PIN, password, or OTP — Nexus Bank staff will NEVER ask for these.
- If you receive a suspicious call, hang up and call 1-800-NEXUS-00 yourself to verify.
- Check the sender email carefully — official Nexus Bank emails come from @nexusbank.com only.

Report phishing:
- Forward suspicious emails to phishing@nexusbank.com.
- Report suspicious SMS to 7726 (SPAM) — this is a free service on most carriers.
- Call 1-800-NEXUS-FR to report fraud attempts.
