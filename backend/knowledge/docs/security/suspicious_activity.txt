Title: Reporting Suspicious Activity on Your Account
Category: security
Doc-ID: sec-006

If you notice anything unusual on your account — transactions you don't recognize, login alerts from unknown devices, or unexpected changes — report it immediately.

Signs of account compromise:
- Transactions you did not make (even small ones — fraudsters often test with small amounts first).
- A login alert from an unknown device or location.
- Your password no longer works.
- You stop receiving bank notifications or statements.
- Unexpected changes to your email address, phone number, or address.
- A 2FA code arrives that you did not request.

What to do immediately:
1. Change your online banking password (if you can still log in).
2. Block any cards you believe may be compromised.
3. Call 1-800-NEXUS-FR (24/7 Fraud Line) immediately.
4. Do not transfer money to any "safe account" suggested by a third party — this is a known scam tactic. Nexus Bank will never ask you to do this.

What Nexus Bank will do:
- Place a temporary security hold on your account while we investigate.
- Review recent account activity.
- Issue new cards with new numbers if needed.
- Work with you to recover any funds lost to fraud, subject to our investigation findings and applicable consumer protection laws.

If a large amount has been transferred fraudulently:
- We may be able to recall the payment if it was made recently. The sooner you report it, the higher the chance of recovery.
- We coordinate with recipient banks on your behalf.

Keep records of: when you noticed the suspicious activity, what transactions are affected, and any communications you received related to the incident.
