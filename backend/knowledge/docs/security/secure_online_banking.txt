Title: Best Practices for Secure Online Banking
Category: security
Doc-ID: sec-004

Following these best practices will significantly reduce the risk of your Nexus Bank account being compromised.

Password security:
- Use a password that is at least 12 characters long with a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.
- Do not use personal information (name, birthday, address) in your password.
- Use a unique password for your banking account — do not reuse it on other websites.
- Change your password every 6-12 months or immediately if you suspect a breach.
- Consider using a reputable password manager (e.g., 1Password, Bitwarden).

Device and network security:
- Only log in on devices you own and trust.
- Keep your operating system, browser, and apps updated — updates include critical security patches.
- Use a VPN when connecting over public Wi-Fi (coffee shops, airports).
- Never access online banking on a shared or public computer.
- Enable full-disk encryption on your laptop and phone.

Session management:
- Always log out after each banking session.
- Do not leave your session open in an unattended browser tab.
- If your session times out, check for any in-progress transactions before re-logging in.

Safe browsing:
- Always ensure the URL starts with "https://" and shows the padlock icon before entering your credentials.
- Bookmark bank.nexusbank.com instead of searching for it — search results can be manipulated to show fake sites.
- Browser extensions can intercept banking sessions — only install extensions from trusted sources and consider using a dedicated browser for banking.

App security:
- Only download the Nexus Bank app from the official App Store or Google Play.
- Enable biometric lock on the app.
- Enable the app's auto-lock setting so it locks after 30 seconds of inactivity.
